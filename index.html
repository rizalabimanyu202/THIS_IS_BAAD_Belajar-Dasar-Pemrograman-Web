    PROFILE

    Baad is a Japanese rock band that formed in 1992 and debuted in February 1993 under label Zain Records, currently signed under Giza Studio. As of 2023, its members are vocal Kyouji Yamada, drummer Kazuyoshi Aoki, guitarist Shinichiro Ohta, and bassist Kobayashi Masamichi.
    The band formation has been completed in 1992 and in 1993 debut single with the single "Donna Toki Demo Hold Me Tight". Their biggest hit came with the single "Kimi ga Suki da to Sakebitai" that was used in the anime series Slam Dunk. The main vocalist and lyricist Yamada Kyouji left the band in 1995 and replaced by Hata Hideki as a second vocalist. Following that, the band switched labels from ZAIN Records to Nippon Columbia. Due to the popularity and sale declines, the band disbanded in 1999. After disband, Ohta and Arai has formed independent rock-band Rad Hammer on the same year and in 2001 has launched their activities as a band, Hata become front vocalist of the independent band Arrow. In 2004, Arai has become member of the independent pop back 03Band and Ohta has become member of the rock band Doa and is part of the band as of 2023.
    In April 2023, the band announced reunite with the former members of the first vocalist and along with announcement of their live concert. On 22 May, a few days after reunited first live, drummer Arai died due to cancer. Two months later, Ohta and doa's members have announced departure from the label Giza Studio and from September and became freelancers under Floodlight company, the announcement was made on their official website.
    In October 2023, Ohta himself as representative of Baad, made guest appearance at the Japan's Anime Song Festival held at Melaka, Malaysia. It became his first stage appearance in oversea since his debut.

    SINGLE

    「どんな時でもHold Me Tight」


    どんな時でもHold Me Tight is BAAD's first debut single, released on February 17, 1993.
    Opening theme for TV Asahi's 「君といつまでも」. Although only Kyoji Yamada appears on the jacket, all members appeared on TV when they sang the title song. At the beginning of the intro, there is the programming sound of Masao Akashi, followed by member Shinichiro Ota's guitar solo, and the coupling song「眩しいRomance」begins with Takeshi Hayama's guitar solo.

    Setlist:
    1. どんな時でもHold Me Tight

    「愛したい愛せない」


    愛したい愛せない is BAAD's second single, released on May 26, 1993.

    「君が好きだと叫びたい」


    君が好きだと叫びたい is BAAD's third single, released on Desember 1, 1993.
    The title song was used as the opening theme for the first half of TV Asahi's anime series 『SLAM DUNK』 BAAD's biggest hit song.
    The instrumental of this song was used in the next preview of the anime, and continued to be used even after it became the opening theme for the second half,『ぜったいに 誰も』.
    According to Kyoji Yamada, the vocalist and lyricist, Takehiko Inoue, the original author of the manga 『SLAM DUNK』, offered to write the song with the image of "hotness" and "sweat," and the lyrics for the A and B verses were written immediately. However, while struggling with the C melody for the chorus, the director suggested, ``Why don't you put more lyrics into the melody at the beginning of the chorus and put the words directly on it?'' It is said that the word ``tai'' was born. In the chorus, "Let's change tomorrow", the live performance includes "Let's try scratching the morning glory," "Let's brush the sea lion's teeth," "Let's buy a soup roll," "Dan Ikeda and Keizo Kanie," Sometimes he sings songs with improvised lyrics, such as ``Let's take a bite of a pumpkin seed.''
    On March 1, 2021, it was selected as the User Voting Award (1989-1999) in Sony Music Entertainment's anime song popularity voting campaign ``Heisei Anime Song Award.'' In the same year, Ayaka Sasaki (Momoiro Clover Z) covered it as a single.

    Setlist:
    1. 君が好きだと叫びたい
    2. TAKE MY DESIRE
    3. 君が好きだと叫びたい（オリジナル・カラオケ）
